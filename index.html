Intro

Hello there! I'm a Civil Engineering graduate who's ventured into the dynamic and ever-evolving world of Machine Learning and AI. My journey here wasn't a straight path, though. I was once on the cusp of diving into a PhD in Civil Engineering, but a burning question changed my course: "What's an exciting and future-proof career where I can harness cutting-edge technologies to build a futuristic world?" AI and advanced machine learning were the answers that resonated with me. While I was managing a lab and dabbling in machine learning, something just clicked. This was what I wanted my future to look like. So, I made a bold move. Instead of pursuing that PhD, I decided to leap into a Master's in Computer Science. Sure, I had my doubts back then—was I making the right choice? Now, looking back, I realize that studying computer science was one of my best decisions. In this portfolio, you'll see my journey, my projects, and the passion I bring to this field. Welcome to my world, where engineering meets AI, and where every challenge is an opportunity to innovate and grow!.

Projects

Integrating Sentiment with LSTM for Enhanced Stock Market Prediction Accuracy

This project applies LSTM models to predict stock prices by leveraging both historical price indicators (like SMA, EMA, volume, and OBV) and sentiment data. Various LSTM architectures were tested, with a single-layer, 50-hidden-unit model (using dropout) performing best. Although wavelet denoising and sentiment features were explored, the biggest gains came from carefully tuning the LSTM and using relevant technical indicators. Overall, the results show that LSTM can effectively capture complex time-series patterns and provide resonably accurate stock price predictions. Click here for Report.

Fine Tunned BERT for NLP Task

I fine-tuned a BERT model for NLP tasks—specifically text classification—achieving an accuracy of 91.51% and an F1-score of 91.78%. By leveraging transfer learning and carefully tuning hyperparameters, this approach adapts the original BERT architecture to new datasets, capturing domain-specific nuances and delivering robust, real-world performance.
